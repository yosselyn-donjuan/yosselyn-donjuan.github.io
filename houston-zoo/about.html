About the Houston Zoo

The Houston Zoo is one of the most visited zoos in the United States, located inside Hermann Park near the heart of the city. It cares for thousands of animals and welcomes millions of guests each year, connecting people with wildlife and inspiring action to save animals in the wild.

Over the years, the zoo has grown from a small collection of animals into a modern conservation organization. Today, it partners with projects around the world to protect species and habitats, while also providing a fun and educational experience for guests of all ages.

Highlights

Key Attractions

African Forest

Walk through a lush habitat featuring gorillas, chimpanzees, and other African wildlife in a shaded, immersive setting.

Asian Elephant Habitat

Watch elephants roam, bathe, and interact with their herd in a spacious habitat designed for their comfort and care.

Kipp Aquarium

Explore underwater worlds filled with colorful fish, coral, and other aquatic species from around the globe.

Texas Wetlands

Discover native Texas species like alligators, whooping cranes, and bald eagles in a habitat inspired by local ecosystems.

Children’s Zoo

Younger guests can get closer to animals, climb, play, and learn through hands-on experiences and interactive spaces.

Seasonal Events

Enjoy special events such as Zoo Lights and themed weekends that transform the zoo into a unique experience.
